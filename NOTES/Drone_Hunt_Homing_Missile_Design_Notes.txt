Drone Hunt - Homing Missile Design Notes

Core Gameplay Loop

-   Select Missile weapon.
-   Hold Right Mouse Button over a drone.
-   Lock meter fills while aiming at the same drone.
-   Lock acquired.
-   Press Left Mouse Button to fire.
-   Homing missile launches.
-   Drone stops its current behavior.
-   Drone attempts to evade.
-   Missile homes toward the target.
-   Missile either hits or misses.

Lock-On

-   Weapon type: MISSILE
-   Hold RMB to lock.
-   Cursor must stay on the same drone.
-   Lock time about 0.8 seconds.
-   Moving away resets or drains lock.
-   Store the locked drone until fired or lock is broken.

Suggested feedback: beep… beep… beep… BEEEEEP (LOCKED)

Homing Missile

Scene: - Sprite2D - CollisionShape2D - GPUParticles2D -
VisibleOnScreenNotifier2D

Behavior: - Starts large near the camera. - Shrinks to the drone’s
apparent size while travelling. - Accelerates. - Turns smoothly like
Mega Man X homing missiles. - Explodes on impact. - Self-destructs if
the target is destroyed.

Drone Evasion

Drone receives: begin_missile_evasion(missile)

BehaviorController pauses normal behavior and performs evasive
maneuvers.

Future evade behaviors: - Break Left - Break Right - Corkscrew - Dive -
Climb - Juke - Hide Behind Cover - Last-second dodge

First implementation: - Random break left/right - Brief pause - Reverse
direction

Resume normal behavior after the missile expires or misses.

Scripts

Modify: - GunManager.gd - Drone.gd - BehaviorController.gd

Create: - HomingMissile.gd - HomingMissile.tscn

Design Goals

-   Missiles should not guarantee a hit.
-   Skilled drones should sometimes evade successfully.
-   Normal gun remains useful.
-   Locking should feel satisfying.
-   Missile should visually shrink toward the target.
-   Drone evasion should look cinematic.
